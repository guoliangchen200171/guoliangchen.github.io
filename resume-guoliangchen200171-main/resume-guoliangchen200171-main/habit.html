Coach

Steve Kerr

Steve Kerr, a graduate of the University of Arizona, is a former American professional basketball player and the current head coach of the Golden State Warriors. He also serves as the head coach of the USA Men's National Basketball Team. In the 1988 NBA Draft, Kerr was selected by the Phoenix Suns in the second round, 25th overall. During his playing career, he played for the Phoenix Suns, Cleveland Cavaliers, Orlando Magic, Chicago Bulls, San Antonio Spurs, and Portland Trail Blazers. He achieved three consecutive championships with the Bulls in the 1995-96, 1996-97, and 1997-98 seasons and won another championship with the Spurs in the 1998-99 season. In the 2002-03 season, he secured another championship with the Spurs before announcing his retirement. In May 2014, Kerr became the head coach of the Golden State Warriors. On April 14, 2016, he led the Warriors to 73 wins in a single season, setting an NBA record. Kerr is the only person to achieve two seasons with 72 wins or more. On December 21, 2021, he became the new head coach of the USA Men's Basketball Team. On June 17, 2022, Kerr led the Warriors to their fourth NBA championship.
